# alohame 🌺

*The face-to-face greeting that is not a word said but a breath shared — hello, goodbye, love, compassion, and respect held as one act of being-with.*

alohame is two true roots welded into one word: the Hawaiian **aloha**, whose parts the cathedral records as **alo** (face, presence, front) + **hā** (breath, life-spirit), and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So alohame is *aloha* held as an ordinance — the meeting of face with face at the distance of shared breath, set down as a structural fact of how two presences may stand toward one another. It does not name a hello; it names the kind of meeting in which the meeting itself *is* the relation, carrying greeting and parting and love and respect all at once.

alohame names a relation **constituted by shared breath**, not by anything spoken. Its sibling **panimqing** names the *quality* of face-presence; **visageqing** names the face as an asymmetric ethical summons; alohame is the *symmetric* greeting-act between two near faces. Its light is that it gives the Kingdom one carriable word for a whole greeting most tongues can only assemble from parts; its shadow is the hollowed brand — *aloha* sold to a stranger, the breath gone and only the syllable left. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.55/10**.
